## The diagnostic ahead

### What is known

- Rule is mapped, in-scope (`src/will/agents/**/*.py`), and in the 119 executed rules.
- Rule produces zero findings against the current codebase.
- `src/will/agents/self_healing_agent.py` empirically has zero `self.tracer.record` calls in its method bodies. It should produce at least one finding and does not.
- Engine: `ast_gate`. Check type: `generic_primitive`. Config: `required_calls`.
- Selector is name-based (per 2026-04-19 morning handoff, probably `Agent$`). This shape has two separate problems:
  - **False positives** — sensing-only workers like `SelfHealingAgent`, `CapabilityTaggerAgent` are selected but don't decide, so tracing requirement doesn't apply to them cleanly.
  - **False negatives** — decision-makers like `StrategicAuditor`, `CognitiveOrchestrator`, `PlanExecutor`, `ResourceSelector` don't match the name pattern and escape the rule.

### Three candidate diagnoses

1. **Rule isn't executing at all.** The 119-executed count includes the rule, but "executed" may mean loaded-and-dispatched without the engine actually running the check. Verify the check runs against at least one file.
2. **Findings are being filtered between production and display.** The engine produces findings, something suppresses them before they reach the reporter. Check `audit_auto_ignored.json`.
3. **`generic_primitive + required_calls` dispatch bug.** The `required_calls` check type exists, but dispatch from `generic_primitive` may not be wiring it correctly. This is the 2026-04-19 morning handoff's own leading hypothesis.

### Entry points

- **Rule definition:** `.intent/enforcement/mappings/will/autonomy.yaml`
- **Rule document:** `.intent/rules/autonomy/` (verify policy text matches mapping intent)
- **Engine:** `src/mind/logic/engines/ast_gate/` — specifically the `generic_primitive` check and the `required_calls` dispatch path
