# Electronic Signature Policy

**Document ID:** SYS-POL-003
**Version:** 1.4
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-20
**Owner:** QA Director
**Classification:** GxP Controlled Document

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy governs the use of electronic signatures (e-signatures) in GxP operations at PharmaCo Ltd. Electronic signatures, when used in conformance with this policy, carry the same legal and regulatory force as handwritten ink signatures and are the binding equivalent of a traditional signature.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who apply electronic signatures to GxP records within PharmaCo systems.

## 3. Electronic Signature Requirements

### 3.1 General Principles

An electronic signature executed within a PharmaCo GxP system has the same impact as a handwritten signature. Each electronic signature is:

- Unique to one individual — electronic signatures shall not be shared, transferred, or used by any person other than the genuine owner
- Applied only by the owner: the signatory shall confirm their intent by entering their unique credentials at the time of signing

### 3.2 Identification Components

PharmaCo employs non-biometric electronic signatures. Each electronic signature shall consist of at least two distinct identification components:

- A unique identification code (user ID) assigned exclusively to a single individual
- A password or passphrase known only to that individual

Both components shall be entered at the time of signing. The system shall require re-entry of credentials for each separate signing act; single sign-on shall not extend to e-signature application.

### 3.3 Signature Manifestations

Every signed electronic record shall display the following information adjacent to or as part of the signature:

- The printed name of the signatory
- The meaning of the signature (e.g. "Reviewed by", "Approved by", "Manufactured by", "Verified by")

These manifestations shall be generated automatically by the system and shall not be modifiable by the signatory after the signature is applied.

### 3.4 Non-Repudiation and Record Integrity

Each electronic signature shall be permanently associated with its signed record. The system shall prevent any signature from being excised from, or otherwise detached from, the record to which it was applied.

### 3.5 Delegated Signing

Electronic signature authority shall not be delegated. No individual may apply an electronic signature on behalf of another, regardless of authorisation. In circumstances where a signatory is unavailable, business processes shall be escalated to an appropriately authorised alternative individual who signs under their own credentials.

## 4. Prohibited Uses

The following are strictly prohibited:

- Sharing logon credentials or passwords with another person for any purpose, including signing
- Applying a signature to a record the signatory has not personally reviewed
- Applying a signature under duress, false pretence, or misrepresentation
- Attempting to transfer or copy an electronic signature to a different record

## 5. Regulatory Basis

This policy implements 21 CFR Part 11 Subpart C (Electronic Signatures, §§11.50, 11.100, 11.200) and EudraLex Volume 4 Annex 11, Provision 14 (Electronic Signature). Any conflict between this policy and applicable regulation shall be resolved in favour of the regulation.

## 6. Training

All personnel authorised to apply electronic signatures shall complete the mandatory e-signature training module before access is granted and at least annually thereafter. Completion shall be recorded in the PharmaCo Training Management System.
