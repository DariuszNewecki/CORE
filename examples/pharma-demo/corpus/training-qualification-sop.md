# Personnel Training and Qualification SOP

**Document ID:** HR-SOP-001
**Version:** 3.0
**Effective Date:** 2026-01-01
**Owner:** Quality Systems Manager
**Classification:** GxP Controlled Document

---

## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines the training and qualification requirements for personnel involved in the development, maintenance, or use of GxP computerised systems at PharmaCo Ltd. It ensures that all such personnel have the education, training, and experience required to perform their assigned tasks competently.

## 2. Scope

This SOP applies to all PharmaCo employees, contractors, and vendors who:

- Develop or program computerised systems used in GxP operations
- Maintain, administer, or support such systems
- Use GxP computerised systems to create, modify, review, or approve electronic records
- Perform validation or quality assurance activities on computerised systems

## 3. Qualification Requirements

### 3.1 Initial Qualification

Prior to gaining access to a GxP computerised system for any operational purpose, a person shall be qualified through at least one of the following:

- Formal education or degree in a relevant discipline, documented and verified by HR
- Demonstrated work experience relevant to the assigned responsibilities, confirmed by the line manager
- Successful completion of role-specific training with a competency assessment

Qualification records shall be established in the PharmaCo Training Management System (TMS) before system access is granted. The System Owner shall confirm completion of qualification before authorising access provisioning.

### 3.2 System-Specific Training

Each GxP computerised system shall have a defined training curriculum appropriate to the user's role. Curricula shall include as a minimum:

- SOPs governing electronic record creation and management for the system
- Electronic signature requirements and the individual's responsibilities
- Data integrity expectations and prohibited practices
- How to escalate anomalies or potential data integrity concerns

Training shall be completed before initial access is granted and shall be refreshed when any of the following occur:

- A major system update or process change affecting the user's role
- A finding or observation related to the user's performance on the system
- Annually as a standing qualification refresh

### 3.3 Competency Assessment

Training completion alone does not constitute qualification. Where the risk level of the task warrants it, a competency assessment (written, practical, or both) shall be completed and passed before the individual is authorised to act independently.

Assessment results shall be documented in the TMS. Individuals who do not pass the assessment shall not be granted access until they complete remedial training and pass a re-assessment.

## 4. Training Records

Training records shall be maintained in the TMS and shall include for each completed training event:

- Employee name, ID, and job title
- Course or SOP identifier and version
- Training delivery method (classroom, eLearning, on-the-job)
- Date completed
- Assessor name and result (if competency-assessed)
- Trainer or supervisor signature (electronic or handwritten)

Training records are GxP records subject to the document retention requirements in QMS-SOP-015.

## 5. Third Parties and Contractors

Third-party and contract personnel shall meet the same training and qualification standards as PharmaCo employees for the systems they access. Evidence of qualification shall be reviewed by IT Compliance and QA before access is granted. Where third-party personnel perform vendor support activities, qualification evidence from the vendor organisation may be accepted subject to a documented review.

## 6. Regulatory Basis

This SOP implements 21 CFR Part 11 §11.10(i) (Personnel qualifications and training for closed systems) and supports Annex 11, Provision 2 (Personnel).
